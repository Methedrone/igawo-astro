# Tax Deadlines 2025/2026 – Filing Calendar

Taxes aren't just about numbers. They're also about deadlines you need to know to avoid losing your refund or facing penalties. In this article, I've gathered all important dates for 2025 and 2026 – for both German and Dutch filings.

## Why Deadlines Matter

Many people think: "Since I'm owed a refund, what's the risk?" In theory, nothing – if you overpaid tax, the office will still refund you even if you file late.

But in practice:

- The office may refuse your return if 4 years (Germany) or 5 years (Netherlands) have passed
- Late refunds take longer – the office prioritizes on-time filings
- If it turns out you owe money (not a refund), late penalties are high
- Some deductions and allowances require claiming within specific deadlines

Know your deadlines = protect your money.

## German Tax Calendar

### Tax Return Filing Deadlines

**For tax year 2024:**

- 31 July 2025 – deadline for self-filers
- 31 May 2025 – deadline for mandatory filers
- 28 February 2026 – deadline if using a tax advisor (Steuerberater)

**For tax year 2025:**

- 31 July 2026 – deadline for self-filers
- 31 May 2026 – mandatory filing deadline
- 28 February 2027 – deadline with a tax advisor

**Note**: if you're not required to file (e.g., only one employer, no additional income), but want to claim a refund, you can file anytime – latest by the end of the fourth year after the tax year.

### Kindergeld Application Deadlines

Applications submitted in 2025 cover periods from January 2025 back to July 2024. There's no strict final deadline, but Kindergeld installments are usually paid from the application date, not earlier.

### SOKA-BAU Holiday Capital Payout Deadline

Holiday capital must be withdrawn by **31 March of the year following** the end of employment. Example: if you finished work in December 2024, you have until 31 March 2025. After that, the money expires.

### Finanzamt Decision Appeal Deadlines

If you receive a decision from the tax office, you have **one month** to file an objection (Widerspruch). The deadline runs from the date of delivery, not the date of issue.

### Tax Return Correction Deadlines

You can file a correction (Berichtigung) anytime until the tax office closes your case. After a final decision, correction becomes harder.

## Dutch Tax Calendar

### Tax Return Filing Deadlines

**For tax year 2024:**

- 1 May 2025 – deadline for self-filers
- 1 September 2025 – deadline if using a tax advisor

**For tax year 2025:**

- 1 May 2026 – self-filing deadline
- 1 September 2026 – deadline with a tax advisor

**Statute of limitations**: 5 years from the end of the tax year. This means in 2025, you can still file for 2020–2024.

### Tax Refund Claim Deadlines

Same as the filing deadline. But as in Germany – if you're owed a refund, the office usually accepts late filings without penalties.

### Belastingdienst Decision Appeal Deadlines

You have **6 weeks** from the date of delivery to file a direct appeal (bezwaar). This is shorter than in Germany, so you need to react quickly.

## Important Regulation Changes for 2025/2026

### Germany

- **Grundfreibetrag (basic allowance)** rises to EUR 12,096 in 2025
- **Commuter rate** (Entfernungspauschale) remains 30 cents per kilometer
- **Kindergeld** stays at EUR 250 per month
- **Electronic filing**: more offices require electronic communication through ELSTER

### Netherlands

- **Heffingskorting** (basic credit) is annually inflation-adjusted
- **Arbeidskorting** (employed person's credit) rises proportionally with wages
- **Wet inkomstenbelasting**: minor changes to progressive tax rates

## What If You Miss a Deadline?

### Germany

If you're owed a refund:

- File as soon as possible
- The office won't charge penalties if they owe you money
- But don't wait too long – the 4-year limit is strict

If you owe money:

- Late penalty is up to 10% of the owed tax per year
- Plus interest
- In extreme cases, a fine

### Netherlands

Similar to Germany – no penalties for refunds, but interest and potential fines for underpayments.

## How to Keep Track of Deadlines

### Set phone reminders

Don't rely on memory. Set reminders one month in advance.

### Create a document list at the start of the year

In January, gather all needed documents. The earlier you start, the less stress.

### Use a tax advisor

If you use an accountant, deadlines are longer (until February/end of year). This gives more time and less stress.

### Check previous years

Have outstanding years? Don't wait. Every year that passes is lost money.

## Summary

Knowing deadlines is the foundation of effective tax filing. Key dates to remember:

- **Germany**: 31 July (self), 28 February (with advisor)
- **Netherlands**: 1 May (self), 1 September (with advisor)
- **SOKA-BAU**: 31 March after ending work
- **Kindergeld**: apply as early as possible
- **Appeals**: 1 month (Germany), 6 weeks (Netherlands)

Don't let a deadline slip past you. A lost refund is money you'll never get back.
