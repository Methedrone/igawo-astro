# SOKA-BAU Leave and Pay Guide for Construction Workers in Germany

If you worked in the German construction industry, you've probably heard of SOKA-BAU. But did you know you might have money waiting to be claimed? Many foreigners leave Germany without realizing they've left hundreds, often thousands, of euros behind.

In this article, I explain what SOKA-BAU is, how the German construction holiday system works, and how to get your money back.

## What Is SOKA-BAU?

SOKA-BAU stands for **Sozialkasse der Bauwirtschaft** – the social fund of the German construction industry. It's an institution that collects contributions for holidays and wages for construction workers.

Unlike standard German leave rules under BUrlG (Federal Leave Act), construction has a special system. Your employer doesn't pay you holiday money directly. Instead, they contribute to SOKA-BAU, which then pays your holiday allowance.

### Why this system?

Construction is a high-turnover industry. Workers frequently change employers and work short contracts. A standard leave system wouldn't work because each employer would only pay for the time worked with them – which with three-month contracts would be absurd.

SOKA-BAU solves this by collecting contributions from all employers and paying holiday pay directly to the worker, regardless of how many companies they've worked for.

## How Much Does the Employer Contribute?

In 2024, construction employers contribute to SOKA-BAU:

- **Holiday contribution**: around 15.4% of gross wages
- **Wage continuation contribution**: around 13% of gross wages

These are additional contributions – not deducted from your salary, paid by the employer. But legally, it's your money – your leave and your holiday capital.

## Who Is Covered by SOKA-BAU?

Not all construction site workers are covered. Contribution obligations apply to:

- Workers performing construction work (including finishing, installation, demolition)
- Workers employed in Germany
- People employed under an employment contract or posted worker arrangements

**Who is not covered:**

- Self-employed (Freiberufler)
- Office and administrative staff (if not doing construction work)
- Workers posted for less than a few weeks (depending on the agreement)

Not sure if your employer contributed? You can check.

## How to Claim Money from SOKA-BAU

There are two main situations where you can get money:

### 1. Taking holiday

If you want to take paid leave, you report it to SOKA-BAU. The fund then pays your holiday allowance (Urlaubsentgelt) for the days you're entitled to.

### 2. Payout of holiday capital after ending employment

If you're finishing work in German construction and don't plan to continue in the industry in Germany, you can apply for payout of accumulated holiday capital.

**Important**: holiday capital is not paid automatically. You must submit an application. Many workers don't do this, and the money is lost.

### Step-by-step application

#### Step 1: Check your balance

Log in to the SOKA-BAU website (soka-bau.de) or call them. To check your balance, you need:

- Social security number (Sozialversicherungsnummer)
- Date of birth
- Registered address in Germany

You can also ask your employer how much they've contributed on your behalf.

#### Step 2: Get the application form

The form is called **Antrag auf Auszahlung des Urlaubskapitals**. It's in German. You can download it from the SOKA-BAU website.

#### Step 3: Fill out the application

Applications are complicated. You must enter:

- Personal details
- All construction employers
- Employment periods
- Social security number
- Bank details

**Most common mistake**: not listing all employers. If you worked for three companies, you must list all three. A missing employer means missing contributions.

#### Step 4: Gather attachments

You need to attach:

- Copy of passport or ID card
- Copies of employment contracts or certificates
- Bank account number in IBAN format
- Any documents confirming employment periods

#### Step 5: Send the application

Send the completed application by post to SOKA-BAU. Keep a copy for yourself.

## How Much Can You Get Back?

It depends on several factors:

- How many months you worked in German construction
- What your salary was
- How many holiday days you used

**Example**: if you worked 6 months at EUR 2,500 gross per month, your employer contributed about EUR 2,300 in holiday contributions. If you didn't use any leave, that money is yours.

In practice, many people recover between EUR 500 and 3,000. I've seen cases where, after many years in construction, the holiday capital exceeded EUR 5,000.

## What About Sick Pay?

SOKA-BAU doesn't just collect holiday contributions – it also finances sick pay. If you were on sick leave and your employer didn't pay full wages, SOKA-BAU may top up the difference.

This is another situation where foreign workers lose money. Many don't know that after six weeks of illness, it's not the employer but SOKA-BAU that pays wages.

## Common Problems with SOKA-BAU

### Employer didn't contribute

This is the most serious problem. If your employer didn't contribute to SOKA-BAU, the fund doesn't have your money. But – you have the right to demand holiday pay directly from your employer. It's a harder route, but possible.

### Missing social security number

If you don't have a Sozialversicherungsnummer, you can't submit an application. You can recover the number through the German social security office or through our firm.

### Changed address

SOKA-BAU sends decisions to your registered address in Germany. If you've already moved abroad, you may not receive correspondence. Always provide a current contact address.

### Deadlines

You must apply for payout of holiday capital by the end of March of the year following the end of employment. After that deadline, the capital expires.
